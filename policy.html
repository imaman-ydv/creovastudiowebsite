Creova Studio – Cookies Policy

(Last Updated: [27/08/2025])
This Cookies Policy explains how Creova Studio (“Company,” “we,” “us,” or “our”) uses cookies and similar technologies to recognize you when you visit our website, use our services, or interact with our content online. It details what these technologies are, why we use them, and the choices you have regarding their use. By continuing to browse our website or use our services, you consent to our use of cookies in accordance with this policy.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device (computer, smartphone, or tablet) when you visit a website. They help websites function efficiently, improve user experience, and provide analytical insights. Cookies may store information such as login credentials, preferences, browsing activity, and session identifiers.

In addition to cookies, we may use other tracking technologies such as:

  • Web Beacons/Pixels – Tiny graphic images that monitor website engagement.
  • Local Storage – Data stored in your browser to remember settings.
  • Session Storage – Temporary data that is cleared when the browser is closed.
  • Tracking URLs – Custom links that monitor click activity.

2. Types of Cookies We Use

We categorize our cookies into several groups:

  1. (a) Strictly Necessary Cookies
    These are essential for the operation of our website and services. They allow you to navigate the website, access secure areas, and use basic functions (e.g., shopping cart, login forms). Without these cookies, the website cannot function properly.
  2. (b) Performance & Analytics Cookies
    These cookies collect information about how visitors use our website (e.g., which pages are most visited, how long users stay, error reports). We use them to monitor performance, analyze trends, and optimize website functionality. Example: Google Analytics.
  3. (c) Functional Cookies
    Functional cookies enable us to remember your choices and preferences (e.g., language, region, theme settings, saved sessions). These enhance your experience by providing personalized content.
  4. (d) Targeting & Advertising Cookies
    These cookies track your browsing habits to deliver relevant ads and measure the effectiveness of advertising campaigns. They may be set by us or third-party networks (such as Google Ads, Facebook Pixel, LinkedIn Insight Tag). These cookies build a profile of your interests and help us deliver customized marketing content.
  5. (e) Social Media Cookies
    These cookies allow you to share content through platforms like Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn, or Twitter. They also track your interactions with embedded content and social plugins.

3. Why We Use Cookies

  • Ensure the technical functionality of our website.
  • Personalize your browsing experience.
  • Measure website traffic, usage, and performance.
  • Deliver targeted marketing and advertising campaigns.
  • Improve security and detect fraudulent activities.
  • Enhance customer support and communication.
  • Conduct research, analytics, and service development.

By combining cookie data with other information (such as registration details), we may build user profiles for better personalization.

4. Third-Party Cookies

Our website may include cookies from third-party providers, including but not limited to:

  • Google Analytics & Google Ads – Website traffic, remarketing, and conversions.
  • Meta (Facebook/Instagram Pixel) – Ad personalization and campaign measurement.
  • LinkedIn Insight Tag – B2B tracking and audience analytics.
  • Payment Gateways (Stripe, PayPal, Razorpay) – Fraud detection and secure transactions.
  • Video Platforms (YouTube, Vimeo) – Embedded media playback analytics.

5. Duration of Cookies

Cookies can be either:

  • Session Cookies – Temporary cookies that are deleted once you close your browser.
  • Persistent Cookies – Remain stored on your device until they expire or you delete them manually. Expiry periods may range from a few minutes to several years, depending on the cookie type and purpose.

6. Managing and Controlling Cookies

You have full control over cookie preferences:

  • Browser Settings: Most browsers allow you to refuse or delete cookies.
  • Opt-Out Tools: You may opt out of Google Analytics (https://tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout) or targeted advertising networks.
  • Consent Manager: On our website, you may accept or reject non-essential cookies through our cookie banner or settings panel.
  • Do Not Track (DNT): While browsers may offer DNT signals, we do not guarantee full compliance as standards are evolving.

⚠️ Please note: Disabling cookies may impact functionality and limit access to certain features of our Services.

7. Data Privacy & Cookies

Any information collected via cookies is processed in line with our Privacy Policy. We do not use cookies to store sensitive data such as passwords or payment details. However, we may link cookie data with personal information you provide to enhance personalization.

8. International Users

Since our Services may be accessed worldwide, cookie data may be transferred across borders and stored in different jurisdictions. By using our Services, you consent to such transfers.

9. Policy Updates

We may update this Cookies Policy to reflect changes in technology, law, or business practices. Updates will be published on this page with a revised “Last Updated” date. Continued use of our website indicates your acceptance of any changes.
